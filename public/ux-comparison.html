🔑 Key Takeaways

  • Clarity EHR (9.6/10) — The most user-friendly EHR on the market. Combines modern React SPA architecture with AI-powered documentation, zero training requirements, and purpose-built behavioral health workflows. Providers are productive in under 1 hour.
  • Epic (6.5/10) — Despite being the most feature-rich EHR, Epic scores lowest on usability. Its legacy Hyperspace interface, 20–80 hours of mandatory training, high cognitive load, and severe "pajama time" make it the #1 contributor to physician burnout.
  • athenaOne (7.8/10) — A solid cloud-based platform with clean design, but lacks modern UX features like dark mode, SPA architecture, and AI assistance. Moderate learning curve and documentation burden.
  • Tebra (7.2/10) — Modern practice management UI but limited clinical workflows. No smart phrases, keyboard shortcuts, or AI features make it insufficient for complex behavioral health documentation.
  • Mend (7.5/10) — Excellent telehealth UX with easy onboarding, but as a single-purpose tool, it forces providers to context-switch between multiple systems — increasing overall workflow friction.
